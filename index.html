Japan is an island country in East-Asia. Located in the Pacific Ocean, it lies to the east of the Sea of Japan, the east of East China Sea, China, North Korea, South Korea and Russia, stretching from the Sea of Okhotsk in the north to the East China Sea and Taiwan in the south. Japan is a stratovolcanic archipelago of 6,852 islands. The four largest are Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu and Shikoku, which make up about ninety-seven percent of Japan's land area. The country is divided into 47 prefectures in 8 regions. The population of 126 million is the world's tenth largest, Japanese make up 98.5% of Japan's total population.

Japanese Culture

Japanse is a fascinating and multifaceted culture with history that stretch back thousands of years, from the feudal lords in the edo period with samurai, swordsmen, geisha and ninjas to the morden day hi-tech society filled with giant robots, manga, anime and fancy sports cars that we know and love. Despite its technological advancement, Japan has retained much of its traditional culture in all forms of the society from language, to artwork and architectures.

Geisha

Geisha are famous female traditional entertainers, who sing, dance, play traditional Japanese musical instruments and engage their clients with interesting conversation. Today there are still some active Geisha in places such as Kyoto and Kanazawa. Geisha were traditionally trained from young childhood. Geisha houses often bought young girls from poor families and took responsibility for raising and training them. During their childhood, apprentice geisha worked first as maids, then as assistants to the house's senior geisha as part of their training and to contribute to the costs of their upkeep and education.

Manga and Anime

If there is one thing Japanese is famous for, its got to be their production of Manga and Anime which are loved and consumed by all over the globe. Manga books are generally printed in black and white and cover a wide variety of genres and subject matter aimed at both sexes and all ages, not just for young kids. Once a manga have proven their worth by popularity, the natural progression is for an animated TV series created, however manga is not the only source of inspiration for anime.

Anime that have made its mark around the world are the unforgettable Dragon Ball Z, Pokemon and Naruto but prehaps the finest animation to come out of Japan has to be those from Studio Ghibli, the critically acclaimed animator and director Miyazaki Hayao produced some of the most unforgettable movies which captivates all types of auidence around the world. These include Princess Monoke, My Neighbour Totoro and Sprited Away, which won the Oscar for Best Animated Feature at the 75th Academy Awards.

Japanese Cuisine

The traditional cuisine of Japan is based on rice with miso soup and other dishes, with an emphasis on seasonal ingredients. Side dishes often consist of fish, pickled vegetables and vegetables cooked in broth. Fish is common, often grilled but also served raw as sashimi or in sushi. Seafood and vegetables are also deep-fried in a light batter as tempura.

Apart from rice, staples include noodles, such as soba and udon. Japan has many simmered dishes such as fish products in broth called oden, or beef in sukiyaki and nikujaga. Foreign food, in particular Chinese food in the form of noodles in soup called ramen and fried dumplings, gyoza and western food such as curry and hamburger steaks are commonly found in Japan.
